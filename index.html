Where are they located?

The Galapagos Islands are located in the Pacific Ocean some 1,000 km from the Ecuadorian coast. This archipelago and its immense marine reserve is known as the unique ‘living museum and showcase of evolution’. Its geographical location at the confluence of three ocean currents makes it one of the richest marine ecosystems in the world.



What makes the Galapagos Islands unique?

The Galapagos Islands have a unique set of environmental conditions that set them apart from all other island groups in the world. Biologists are fascinated by the island ecosystems. A great example is a world-changing historic visit from a man named Charles Darwin; the Galapagos Islands are quite arguably the most studied archipelago in the world.
